Daphnia pulex

Daphnia pulex is the most common species of water flea and is found throughout the Americas, Europe and Australia. Daphnia pulex occurs in a wide range of aquatic habitats, although it is most closely associated with small, shaded pools. In nutrient-poor bodies of water, Daphnia pulex are usually colorless, but in nutrient-rich waters, their bodies can be tinted red. Dapnia pulex is a very common Cladoceran species in the Delta and is recognizable by its pear-shaped body, posterior spine, and smooth postabdominal claw.Like most other cladoceran, Daphnia pulex are more likely to be found in a body of slow moving water like Wings Landing or other pond sites.
