Acartiella

Acartiella is a less common species of copepod in the Delta, recognizable by its long, cigar-like body morphology, long antennae, and its smooth prosome. These calanoid copepods use their antennae for rapid propulsion through the water and one can distinguish between adults and copepodites based on the presence of a fifth leg or if the individual is gravid.

Acartiella can be found in brackish, open water areas like Montezuma slough. Acartiella can easily be confused with Sinocalanous, another calanoid copepod, but an adult Sinocalanous has small spikes on either side of its inferior prosome while Acartiella do not.
