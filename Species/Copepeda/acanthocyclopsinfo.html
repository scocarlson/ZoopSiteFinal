Acanthocyclops

Acanthocyclops is a very common genus of copepod in the Delta, recognizable by its teardrop shape, short urosome, and short antennae. There are seven different similar-looking species; all of which are native to the area and can be found in both brackish and fresh water. Gravid adult females have two egg sacs are either side of their bodies and one can distinguish between adults and copepodites based on size and the presence of a fifth leg. This Cyclopoid copepod uses its antennae for rapid propulsion through the water and has ridges alongside its abdominal regions to disrupt laminar flow which allows for increased hydrodynamics. An Acanthocyclops sp. can be easily confused with individuals belonging to another Cyclopoid genus, Limnothona, but individuals in the Acanthocyclops genus have shorter urosomes and are on average much bigger than individuals in the Limnothona genus.
