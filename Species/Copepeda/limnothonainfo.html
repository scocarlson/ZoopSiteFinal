Limnothona

Limnothona is a fairly common genus of Copepod in the Delta, recognizable by its teardrop shape, long urosome, and short antennae. These individuals are most commonly found in open areas of brackish water. This species looks similar to the Acanthocylops sp. but have longer urosomes and are generally smaller. Gravid adult females have two egg sacs are either side of their bodies and one can distinguish between adults and copepodites based on size and the presence of a fifth leg. These Cyclopoid Copepods use their antennae for rapid propulsion through the water and have ridges alongside their abdominal regions to disrupt laminar flow which allow for increased hydrodynamics.
