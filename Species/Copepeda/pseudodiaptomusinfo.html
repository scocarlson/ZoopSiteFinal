Pseudodiaptomus

Pseudodiaptomus is a very common genus of copepod in the Delta, recognizable by its sausage-like shape, mid-length antennae, and the shallow split in its urosome giving way to shorter rami. These calanoid copepods use their antennae for rapid propulsion through the water and are most commonly found in warmer seasons. Gravid adult females have two large egg sacs held on either side of their bodies and a nongravid adult female can be distinguished by two triangular shaped plates on either side of its prosome. One can distinguish between adults and copepodites based on the presence of a fifth leg. Pseudodiaptomus sp. are often confused by their colder water counterpart, Eurytemora sp. One can distinguish an adult Pseudodiaptomus sp. from an adult Eurytemora sp. based on how deep the urosome splits.
