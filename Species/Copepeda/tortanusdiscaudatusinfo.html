Tortanus discaudatus

Tortanus discaudatus is a calanoid copepod and can be found in marine environments such as the Chukchi Sea, the Bering Strait, the Sea of Japan, the Sea of Okhotsk, the North Sea, and the Atlantic Coast of North America. T. discaudatus live in the neritic zone of these coastal waters in which the water is so shallow, the ocean floor remains in the photic zone. These individuals are carnivorous as adults and eat copepod larvae and smaller copepods. They are eaten by larger fish and other predators.

T. discaudatus are on average 1.3mm to 2.8mm and females are usually slightly larger than males. They have elongated prosomes with a long and asymmetrical urosome. They can be distinguished from other calanoid copepods by their asymmetrical caudal rami located on the tip of the tail region. Females have curved pointed processes on the end of their fifth body segment while the same region on males appears rounded.
