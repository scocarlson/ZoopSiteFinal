Eurytemora

Eurytemora is a very common genus of Copepod in the Delta, recognizable by its sausage-like shape, medium length antennae, and the deep split in the urosome giving way to longer rami. These Calanoid Copepods use their antennae for rapid propulsion through the water and are most commonly found in winter because they thrive in colder water. Eurytemora sp. was also introduced with striped bass from the East coast and are speculated to be a key factor in the success of the striped bass population on the West coast. Gravid adult females have one large egg sac held below their bodies and one can distinguish between adults and copepodites based on the presence of a fifth leg.
