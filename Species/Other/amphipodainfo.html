Amphipoda

Amphipoda are commonly found in the Delta. Unlike zooplankton, Amphipod species are much larger and are able to be counted with the naked eye. The two families of Amphipods found in the Delta are Gammaridae and Corophiidae and within them are the genera, Gammarus and Corophium. Both types of Amphipods look like small, shrimp-like crustaceans, but move differently from each other. In the field, species of Gammarus crawl with their bodies undulating in a sideways fashion while species of Corophium crawl more upright. In the lab, one can easily distinguish between the two in that any of the four Corophium species in the Delta has enlarged second antennae protruding from its head while any individual of the Gammarus species do not.
