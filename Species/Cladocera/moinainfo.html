Moina

Moina is a less common Cladoceran species in the Delta and is recognizable by its pear-shaped body and a small dangling appendage right next to its second antennae used for feeding. Moina also have flatter heads in comparision to Daphnia pulex, Simocephalus and Diphanosoma, although it does not appear as flat as Ceriodaphnia heads. Like most other Cladoceran, Moina are likely to be found in bodies of slow moving water and are especially known to be found in the Cache-1 ARC site.
