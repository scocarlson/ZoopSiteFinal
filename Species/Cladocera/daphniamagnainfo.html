Daphnia magna

Daphnia magna is a common Cladoceran species in the Delta and is recognizable by its pear-shaped body, posterior spine, dorsal spinules and the divot in its postabdominal claw. Adult males and females are different in that males have two cylindrical protrusions extending from their maxillae. Daphnia magna can easily be confused with Daphnia pulex, but their defining features are the dorsal spinules and the divot in the postabdomoinal claw which Daphnia pulex do not have. Also, Daphnia magna are on average much larger than Daphnia pulex. Like most other cladoceran, Daphnia magna is more likely to be found in a body of slow moving water like Wings Landing or other pond sites.
