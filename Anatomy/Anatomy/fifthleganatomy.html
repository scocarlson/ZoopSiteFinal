Fifth Leg

The fifth leg is one of the most important factors for determining between an adult and a copepodite individual. Adult copepods will have a fully developed fifth leg attached to their prosome which looks a little more crooked than the rest of the legs. the purpose of this leg in male copepods is that it enables a male to latch onto a female during mating. For some species like Eurytemora, the fifth leg helps females support their egg sacs when gravid. Identifying the fifth leg usually takes a keen eye and careful inspection except in the case of an adult female whose bent fifth leg will be immediately apparent.
