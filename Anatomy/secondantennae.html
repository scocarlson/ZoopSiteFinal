Second Antennae

The second antennae is a key characteristic of cladoceran. These appendages are attached to the carapace, or body, and allows for rapid propulsion through water. Each antennae also has numerous swimming hairs that have been speculated to serve some sort of sensory function much like setae on copepods. In species like Diphanosoma and Moina, the width of the superior portion of the second antennae is a distinguishing feature.
