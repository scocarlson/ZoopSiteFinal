Start by considering generalbody morphology.

If your specimen has a narrow opaque body, it is likely part of the subclass, Copepoda. If your specimen has a globular translucent body, it is likely a member of the class Cladocera.

  • Teardrop or sausage-like body morphology
  • Multiple legs
  • Two distinguishable first antennae
  • Globular, translucent body
  • Visible eyespot
  • Two distinguishable second antennae
