Acartia clausii

Defining characteristics are:

  • Row of large spines at the metasome
  • Long first antennae (at least half the length of their bodies)
  • Branched second antennae
  • Joint between the fifth and sixth body segments
  • Sausage-like body morphology

If your specimen has a fifth leg, it is an adult. If not, it is a copepodite. If your specimen has a reddish tint on its urosome, it is a female individual. If an A. clausii is gravid, it will have two eggs sacs on either side of its body. Learn More.
