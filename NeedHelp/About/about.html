About the Guide

The purpose of this guide is to provide an educational resource as well as standardized method of identifying different zooplankton species in the Sacramento-San Joaquin River Delta for the purposes of research at the Center for Watershed Sciences and the greater scientific community. Zooplankton is primarily counted for the Arc of Native Fishes project which quantifies zooplankton for the purpose of gaining a greater understanding of what conditions are best for native fish.

This site was created by Scout Carlson, a 4th year Marine and Coastal Sciences undergraduate student at the University of California at Davis. She has worked in the Biology Lab at the Center for Watersehd Sciences since the summer of 2017 and has spent 1.5 years counting zooplankton for Nicole Aha's Caged Salmon Project and 1 year developing this website for John Durand's ecological research group. She hopes to someday create more tools that aid and optimize the process of scientific research. In her spare time, she also is the president of UC Davis's oldest a cappella group, Lounge Lizards A Cappella and an award-winning choreographer at the 2019 International Championship of Collegiate A Cappella. She also enjoys sustainable fashion, website design, and has an extensive knowledge of rocks and minerals.
