Question: How do I find more information on the species of a specific individual?

Answer: Go to the 'Species' tab in the bar at the top of the page and navigate to either 'Copepod', 'Cladocera', or 'Other' to find images and names of each species.

Question: How do I find more information on the general anatomical terminology written in the species descriptions?

Answer: Navigate to the 'Anatomy' tab in the bar at the top of the page and go to the 'General Anatomy' link directly under the title.

Question: Is there an easier way to count zooplankton than the manual method?

Answer: There have been efforts in the past to automate the process--a 2D imaging system in the 1980s that did not account for random orientation of individuals and still required plating aliquots as well as a technique called ZOOSCAN in 2004 which uses two algorithms to sort out questionable individuals. Both methods have a 90% and 80% accuracy rate respectively, but still can only identify down to genus rather than species. It has been thought that a mix of automated and manual counting is the best approach, but it also depends on the specificity needed in different research scenarios.
